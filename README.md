# Optimizing-the-airline-s-flight-allocation-to-maximize-profits
An airline plans 12 routes with Boeing 737-800 &amp; Airbus A320. Maximize profit: flight count ≤300, total distance ≤750,000 km, ≥8 flights/route, no route exceeds 15% of total flights.

Optimization problem
An airline is planning its operations for the upcoming month. The analysis focuses on the 12 most frequent flight routes. The company operates two types of aircraft: Boeing 737-800 and Airbus A320, which differ in capacity (number of passenger seats) and in the level of fixed costs per flight. Revenue per flight depends on the aircraft type and the route, as the average ticket price varies across different destinations. Fuel costs depend on the flight distance.
The company aims to determine the optimal number of flights to operate on each route during the month in order to maximize total profit, subject to the following constraints:
The total number of flights across all routes for the month cannot exceed 300.
The total flight distance (kilometers flown) for the month must not exceed 750 000 km.
A minimum of 8 flights must be operated on each of the 12 routes.
No single route should account for more than 15% of the total number of flights.
